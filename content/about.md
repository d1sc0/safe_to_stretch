# About the Safe to Stretch Toolkit

This toolkit was developed by [Stuart Mackenzie](https://hellostu.xyz) as part of the Master's programme in Creative Teaching and Learning Design at Arts University Bournemouth.

## 🔍 Linking Primary Research to Design
The design of this toolkit was directly shaped by research into what blocks learning in the workplace. In particular, primary research (surveys, interviews, and observations of professional training groups) showed three key insights, which we have mapped directly to features in this tool:

### 1. The Challenge of "Evaluation Apprehension" (Fear of Being Judged)
*   **Research Insight**: Junior employees regularly stay quiet during training when senior leaders or direct managers are in the same room. They feel the professional risk of asking basic questions or admitting they do not know something is too high.
*   **Design Response**: 
    *   **Asynchronous Digital Prep** ([Before Guidelines](#before)): Recommending anonymous prep tools and digital submission paths before the group meets.
    *   **The Silent Brainstorm** ([During Guidelines](#during)): Setting aside quiet writing time (using post-its or digital walls) so everyone contributes ideas anonymously before any verbal discussion begins. This helps people share without having to speak up first in front of managers.
    *   **Reviewing Participation & Splitting Groups** ([After Guidelines](#after)): Advising designers to track who contributed and, when the topics are highly sensitive, split training groups by seniority.

### 2. The Influence of the Organisation's "Hidden Curriculum"
*   **Research Insight**: Every organisation has implicit, unwritten rules that shape how people speak to each other (such as "don't challenge executive opinions" or "always appear certain"). These habits carry over directly into training sessions.
*   **Design Response**: 
    *   **The Co-Created Charter** ([During Guidelines](#during)): Allocating 10 minutes at the start of a session for the group to co-create their own ground rules. Instead of the facilitator declaring it a "safe space," the group uses **Reverse Brainstorming** (describing the worst possible session) to actively identify and break down these unwritten rules.

### 3. The Power Asymmetry of the Facilitator Role (Teacher-Student Dynamics)
*   **Research Insight**: Learners often view the facilitator as a boss or a perfect "expert," which encourages people to passively agree and discourages them from taking risks.
*   **Design Response**: 
    *   **Facilitator Vulnerability** ([Before Guidelines](#before)): Facilitators are guided to start with activities where they share their own failures, mistakes, or knowledge gaps first, helping to reduce hierarchy and build trust.

## Site Build

This early version of the toolkit is built as a **Single Page Application (SPA)** with zero build tools or dependencies, allowing it to be hosted easily on static environments (in this case GitHub Pages). Content is maintained in markdown files for easy editing and all source code is [maintained in GitHub](https://github.com/d1sc0/safe_to_stretch).
