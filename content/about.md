# About the Safe to Stretch Toolkit

This toolkit was developed by [Stuart Mackenzie](https://hellostu.xyz) as part of the Master's programme in Creative Teaching and Learning Design at Arts University Bournemouth.

## 🔍 Linking Primary Research to Design

The design of this toolkit was directly shaped by research into what blocks learning in the workplace. In particular, primary research (surveys, interviews, and observations of professional training groups) showed three key insights, which we have mapped directly to features in this tool:

### 1. Navigating "Evaluation Apprehension" (Fear of Status Judgement)
*   **Research & Theoretical Insight**: Junior employees regularly mask their vulnerability and stay quiet during training when senior leaders or direct managers are in the same room. Grounded in the "Status" and "Fairness" dynamics of David Rock’s SCARF model, the perceived professional risk of admitting a knowledge gap in front of leadership triggers a cognitive threat response, forcing learners into a defensive posture of self-preservation.
*   **Design Response (UDL: Multiple Means of Engagement, Action & Expression)**:
    *   **Asynchronous Digital Prep** ([Before Guidelines](#before)): Providing low-stakes, varied preparation paths and anonymous digital submission channels before the live cohort meets to lower the entry barrier.
    *   **The Silent Brainstorm** ([During Guidelines](#during)): Allocating quiet writing blocks (using digital post-its or chat bars) so everyone can express ideas anonymously before any verbal discussion begins. This decouples a participant's ideas from their job title or social anxiety.
    *   **Reviewing Participation Dynamics** ([After Guidelines](#after)): Advising designers to critically analyse the quantitative split of contribution channels and, where abstract or identity-centred topics are highly sensitive, split training cohorts purely by seniority to allow safer processing spaces.

### 2. Disrupting the Organisation's "Hidden Curriculum"
*   **Research & Theoretical Insight**: Every workplace possesses implicit, unwritten rules and cultural norms that dictate how people communicate (e.g., "never contradict executive opinion" or "always project absolute certainty"). As illuminated by Paulo Freire’s critique of top-down educational banking systems, these oppressive organisational habits carry over directly into training spaces, transforming an active learning environment back into a passive corporate silo.
*   **Design Response (Dismantling Power Dynamics)**:
    *   **The Co-Created Compact** ([During Guidelines](#during)): Dedicating the first 10 minutes of a high-stakes programme for the cohort to co-create their own ground rules. Rather than the facilitator top-down declaring a space "safe," the group utilises Reverse Brainstorming (collaboratively designing the worst session possible) to actively identify, objectify, and break down their workplace's specific hidden curriculum.

### 3. De-escalating the Power Asymmetry of the Facilitator Role
*   **Research & Theoretical Insight**: Professional learners often reflexively view a facilitator through a traditional "teacher-student" hierarchy—perceiving them as an evaluating authority or an infallible expert. This power asymmetry encourages compliance and passive agreement while actively discouraging the risk-taking necessary for critical thinking.
*   **Design Response (Engaged Pedagogy)**:
    *   **Facilitator Vulnerability-Modelling** ([Before Guidelines](#before)/[During Guidelines](#during)): Rooted in bell hooks’ philosophy of engaged pedagogy, facilitators are guided to start sessions by explicitly sharing their own past professional failures, mistakes, or technical blind spots. By modelling vulnerability from the front, the facilitator intentionally flattens the hierarchy, lowers the cognitive load of perfectionism, and signals that the space is safe for experimentation.

## Site Build & Accessibility

This early version of the toolkit is built as an accessible **Single Page Application (SPA)** with zero build tools or heavy external dependencies, allowing it to be hosted fluidly on static environments (in this case GitHub Pages).

In alignment with UDL digital delivery standards, the system prioritises clean typography, minimal layouts to reduce cognitive load, full compatibility with screen readers, and markdown-maintained content files for absolute flexibility and ease of modification. All open-source code is [maintained in GitHub](https://github.com/d1sc0/safe_to_stretch).
