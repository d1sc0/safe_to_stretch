# About the Safe to Stretch Toolkit

This toolkit was developed by [Stuart Mackenzie](https://hellostu.xyz) as part of the Master's programme in Creative Teaching and Learning Design at Arts University Bournemouth.

## 🔍 Linking Primary Research to Design

The design of this toolkit was directly informed by primary and secondary research into workplace learning inhibitors. In particular, primary research (surveys, interviews, and observations of professional learning cohorts) revealed three critical insights, which we have mapped explicitly to features in the toolkit:

### 1. The Challenge of "Evaluation Apprehension" in Mixed-Seniority Cohorts

- **Research Insight**: Junior employees consistently self-censor during training when senior leaders or direct managers are present in the room. They calculate the professional risk of asking "naive" questions or admitting knowledge gaps as too high.
- **Design Response**:
  - **Asynchronous Digital Infrastructure** ([Before Guidelines](#before)): Recommending anonymous prep tools and digital submission paths before the cohort meets.
  - **The Silent Brainstorm** ([During Guidelines](#during)): Standardizing silent writing windows (using post-its or digital walls) so everyone contributes ideas anonymously before any verbal discussion begins, removing the barrier of having to speak up first in front of managers.
  - **Post-Session Audit & Cohort Separation** ([After Guidelines](#after)): Advising designers to track participation splits and, where transformational risk is high, segment training cohorts by rank.

### 2. The Influence of the Organizational "Hidden Curriculum"

- **Research Insight**: Every organization has implicit, unwritten rules that dictate communication patterns (e.g., "don't challenge executive opinions" or "maintain a veneer of absolute certainty"). These patterns carry over directly into training spaces.
- **Design Response**:
  - **The Co-Creation Blueprint & Charter** ([During Guidelines](#during)): Designing a structured, 10-minute collaborative chartering phase at the start of sessions. Instead of the facilitator declaring it a "safe space," the group co-creates rules to more actively dismantle corporate communication and collaboration habits.

### 3. The Power Asymmetry of the Facilitator Role

- **Research Insight**: Learners frequently view the facilitator as an extension of the organizational hierarchy or an infallible "expert," which reinforces passive compliance and discourages risk-taking.
- **Design Response**:
  - **Educator Vulnerability** ([Before Guidelines](#before)): Facilitators are guided to design opening activities where they actively share their own failures, mistakes, or knowledge gaps first, applying bell hooks' principles of engaged pedagogy.

## Site Build

This early iteration of the toolkit has been built as a **Single Page Application (SPA)** with zero build tools or dependencies, allowing it to be hosted easily on static environments (in this case GitHub Pages). Content is maintained in markdown files for easy editing and all source code is [maintained in GitHub](https://github.com/d1sc0/safe_to_stretch).
