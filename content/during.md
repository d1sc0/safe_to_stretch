# 📢 DURING THE SESSION (Facilitation & Co-Creation)

### 1. The Co-Creation Blueprint

Do not simply announce that "rank is paused" or "this is a safe space"— this can feel like a trap to junior staff. Spend time at the beginning of the sessions co-creating a **Session Charter** to give the group true ownership of the space: You may want to suggest some basic ground rules but build consensus around these and actively seek contributons from the cohort.

- **The Silent Brainstorm:** Before anyone speaks out loud, give options for how people contribute. Give everyone 3 minutes to post anonymously to a digital wall or use physical post-it notes responding to the plain-language prompt: _"What do you need from this room today to feel comfortable testing messy ideas or making mistakes without judgment?"_ This removes the barrier of having to speak up first in a room full of senior peers.
- **The Reverse Brainstorm:** If the group is hesitant, ask the inverse: _"Let’s design the worst possible session. What behaviours in this room today would guarantee that you completely shut down and hide your ideas?"_ Flip their answers (e.g., pulling rank, checking emails) into the official ground rules.
- **The Active Commitment:** Read the compiled rules out loud, look the senior leaders in the eye, and ask: _"Do we collectively agree to these rules, and do I have your explicit permission as a facilitator to gently call it out if we drift away from them?"_ This gives you a clear mandate to manage power dynamics later without looking like you are personally attacking senior staff.

### 2. In-Session Flattening Tactics

- **Offer Multiple Channels for Expression:** Never evaluate engagement purely by who talks the loudest. Allow learners to express their understanding in various ways: verbally, via text chat, through collaborative sketching, or in small breakout duos. Provide tools like a parking lot for learners to anonymously and quietly surface issues, ask questions and give feedback.
- **Manage Airtime Proactively:** Actively interrupt dominant or high-status voices who over-index on the room's conversational space. Utilize silent writing intervals to ensure quieter or lower-ranking participants have equal processing time to contribute.
- **Frame Mistakes as Data:** When a participant shares a failure or a "wrong" answer, explicitly celebrate it as a valuable data point for the group's collective learning.
- **Role modelling:** Role model the desired behaviours through active listening, inquiry, owning and being transparent with mistakes made during the session, challenging behaviour that contradicts the charter agreed.
