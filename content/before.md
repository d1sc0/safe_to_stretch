# 🛑 BEFORE THE SESSION (Design & Preparation)

Planning ahead and setting clear guidelines are crucial to balancing power before learners enter the room. Use these guidelines to check safety needs, map reporting lines, prepare your own mindset, and build fair digital tools.

### 1. The Safety & Accessibility Audit

- **Evaluate the Content Risk:** Is this training practical (low social risk) or transformational (high personal risk)? Increase your safety measures if the topic asks learners to change their mindsets or how they interact with others (interpersonal behaviours).
- **Map the Group Hierarchy:** Identify reporting lines within your attendee list. Expect junior staff to calculate the professional risk of speaking up or asking questions in front of senior managers. When appropriate and possible, consider splitting groups by rank or seniority level to reduce potential tensions.
- **Provide Flexible Pre-Work Options (UDL: Representation):** Do not rely only on reading lists. Share pre-session information in different formats—such as a short video, an audio summary, or a clear diagram. This reduces cognitive load and supports different learning styles.

### 2. The Facilitator’s Internal Landscape

- **Check Your Own Behavioural Triggers:** Reflect on how you react when a participant challenges your expertise, disagrees with a concept, or throws your lesson plan off-script. Spot these triggers early so you can respond with calm curiosity rather than shutting down the room.
- **Model Vulnerability in the Session Design:** Design an opening activity where _you_ share a past professional failure, mistake, or knowledge gap first. This directly applies bell hooks' concept of **engaged pedagogy**—the belief that the educator must share their own vulnerability to reduce classroom hierarchies and build trust: *“empowerment cannot happen if we refuse to be vulnerable while encouraging students to take risks”* (see [bell hooks – Engaged Pedagogy & Vulnerability](#resources)).

### 3. Asynchronous Digital Tools

- **Anonymity as Equity (UDL: Engagement):** If your design includes online or shared digital spaces, make sure to use anonymous tools. This offers low-pressure options for engagement, letting quieter learners or junior staff participate without feeling exposed or limited by their job title.
