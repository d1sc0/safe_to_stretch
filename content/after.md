# 🔄 AFTER THE SESSION (Measurement & Iteration)

Checking how safe people felt and reviewing who participated after the session helps you improve future training designs.

### 1. The Post-Session Safety Audit

- **Analyse the Participation Split:** Review your session notes or digital board inputs. Did lower-ranking staff contribute as much as senior staff across the different channels (writing, sketching, speaking)? If there is a clear difference, consider how improvements to the session design could better balance the workplace hierarchy.
- **Measure Safety Anonymously:** Include a direct, plain-language question in your post-session feedback form: _"On a scale of 1 to 5, how safe did you feel to challenge assumptions, ask basic questions, or admit a mistake in this room?"_
- **Reduce the "Anxiety Hangover":** If a participant was very vulnerable or made a visible mistake during a live session, check in with them briefly and privately afterward to make sure they are not feeling anxious about their professional standing.

### 2. Continuous Learning Design

- **Iterate Your Plan:** Treat your session design as a work in progress. Use the safety feedback to adjust your next training plan—such as changing breakout group sizes, using more anonymous tools, or spending more time on the ground rules at the start.
