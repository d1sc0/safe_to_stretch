# Welcome to the Safe to Stretch Toolkit

When people participate in workplace training, they need to feel safe to speak up, ask questions, and make mistakes. When employees of different levels of seniority train together, workplace hierarchies can limit everyone: junior staff may stay silent to avoid professional risk, while senior leaders may hold back to maintain the expectation that they must always have all the answers.

This toolkit provides prompts and tools for facilitators and learning designers to navigate these dynamics and build psychological safety in any learning environment. It aims to provide simple suggestions to help balance power in the room, making it easier for everyone to participate comfortably.

This is an early version of the toolkit. The guidelines are split into sections reflecting considerations you can take [before](#before), [during](#during), and [after](#after) training.
