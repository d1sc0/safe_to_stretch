# 📚 DESIGNER RESOURCES & UNDERPINNING THEORIES

This collection of resources brings together the key research, frameworks, and practical thinking that shaped the design of this toolkit. They explore the relationships between workplace hierarchy, learning design, and psychological safety. To suit different learning styles, we have included a mix of books, videos, podcasts, and articles for each topic:

### The Universal Design for Learning (UDL) Guidelines

A framework that guides the design of learning environments to ensure they are accessible, flexible, and inclusive for all learners by providing multiple options for engagement, representation, and expression.

- 🌐 **Framework Portal**: [CAST UDL Guidelines Interactive Website](https://udlguidelines.cast.org/)
- 🎥 **Video**: [UDL at a Glance (YouTube)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bDvKnY0g6e4) — A brief, visual introduction explaining the core brain networks involved in learning.
- 🎙️ **Podcast**: [Think UDL Podcast](https://thinkudl.org/) — Hosted by Lillian Nave, exploring practical UDL implementations across education and design.
- 📝 **Blog / Article**: [UDL 3.0 Guidelines Release Context](https://www.cast.org/) — Information on CAST's latest framework updates targeting equity and bias.

### David Rock’s SCARF Model

Explores how choices in learning design can accidentally trigger a defensive threat response in a professional's brain by challenging their **Status**, **Certainty**, **Autonomy**, **Relatedness**, or **Fairness**.

- 📝 **Encyclopedic Overview**: [SCARF Model Overview (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Rock_(consultant)#SCARF_model) — A clean, ad-free explanation of the SCARF framework, its development, the five social domains, and its applications in organisational psychology.
- 📝 **Bitesize Guide**: [The SCARF Model — Bitesize (Tash Willcocks on Medium)](https://tash-willcocks.medium.com/the-scarf-model-bitesize-e04585e156b9) — A practical, quick-read summary of how to recognize SCARF triggers in communication and use them to navigate team dynamics and feedback.
- 🎥 **TEDx Talk**: [Learning About the Brain Changes Everything (YouTube)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uDIyxxayNig) — Dr. David Rock explains the neural drivers of social behaviour at TEDxTokyo.
- 🎙️ **Podcast**: [Your Brain at Work Podcast by NLI (Apple Podcasts)](https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/your-brain-at-work/id1453229871) — Deep-dives into applying neuroscience to leadership and organisational dynamics.
- 📖 **Book**: [_Your Brain at Work_ (Google Books)](https://books.google.com/books?isbn=9780063003156) — David Rock's book on managing cognitive load and social threats.

### Paulo Freire – Pedagogy of the Oppressed

Illustrates how educational approaches are never neutral; they either mirror top-down corporate management styles (the "banking concept" of education) or act as a social fulcrum to empower active, participatory freedom.

- 📖 **Access Book**: [Bloomsbury Publishing](https://www.bloomsbury.com/us/pedagogy-of-the-oppressed-9781501314131/) or read the free online text at [History Is A Weapon](https://www.historyisaweapon.com/defcon2/pedagogy/index.html).
- 🎙️ **Podcast Deep-Dive**: [Philosophize This! Episode 210 (Spotify/Web)](https://philosophizethis.org/podcast/episode-210-the-pedagogy-of-the-oppressed-paulo-freire-education) — Stephen West's breakdown of Freire's critical pedagogy and the banking model of education.
- 🎥 **Video Series**: [Pedagogy of the Oppressed Chapter-by-Chapter (YouTube)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kYJv1rNnJ7Q) — Detailed video analysis by academic Dr. Masood Raja.

### bell hooks – Engaged Pedagogy & Vulnerability

Examines the concept of engaged pedagogy, demonstrating how a facilitator bringing their whole, vulnerable, authentic self into a learning environment is a prerequisite for supporting others in developing genuine critical thinking skills and reducing status-based hierarchies.

- 📖 **Access Book (1994)**: [Teaching to Transgress: Education as the Practice of Freedom (Routledge)](https://www.routledge.com/Teaching-to-Transgress-Education-as-the-Practice-of-Freedom/hooks/p/book/9780415908085) — Focuses on education as a practice of freedom. In it, hooks argues: _“empowerment cannot happen if we refuse to be vulnerable while encouraging students to take risks... Professors who expect students to share confessional narratives but who are themselves unwilling to share are exercising power in a manner that could be coercive.”_
- 📖 **Access Book (2010)**: [Teaching Critical Thinking: Practical Wisdom (Routledge)](https://www.routledge.com/Teaching-Critical-Thinking-Practical-Wisdom/hooks/p/book/9780415968201) — Essential practical teachings on teaching practices, critical thinking, and classroom dialogue.
- 🎥 **Recorded Conversation**: [bell hooks & Chirlane McCray: Critical Thinking at The New School (YouTube)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=G16x0k0fNWU) — Discussion on pedagogy, critical thinking, and healing.
- 🎥 **Video Panel**: ["Education Liberates" with bell hooks and Bettina Love (YouTube)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kY2C_ATNFEM) — Exploration of the radical potential of inclusive education.

### Amy Edmondson’s Psychological Safety

Explores how creating a workplace climate where individuals feel comfortable speaking up, sharing concerns, and asking questions is crucial for learning, innovation, and overall team performance.

- 📖 **Book**: [_The Fearless Organisation_ (Google Books)](https://books.google.com/books?isbn=9781119477242) — The definitive guide on cultivating psychological safety in organisations.
- 🎥 **TEDx Talk**: [Building a Psychologically Safe Workplace (YouTube)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LhoLuui9gX8) — Dr. Amy Edmondson's classic talk at TEDxHGSE.
- 🎙️ **Podcast**: [HBR IdeaCast: Creating Psychological Safety in the Workplace](https://hbr.org/podcast/2019/01/creating-psychological-safety-in-the-workplace) — HBR's interview on why psychological safety is not about "being nice."
- 🎙️ **Adam Grant Podcast**: [WorkLife: Is it Safe to Speak Up at Work? (Apple Podcasts)](https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/is-it-safe-to-speak-up-at-work/id1346314086?i=1000406834015) — Episode focusing on the culture of voice versus silence, featuring Amy Edmondson.

### Facilitation Blogs & Practical Articles

These articles and blog posts from active practitioners share real-world experiences of holding space, managing group dynamics, and building trust. They offer practical ways to address senior authority, handle conflict, and use silence to make learning environments safer for everyone:

- 📝 **Article**: [The Messy Middle: Holding Space Through Kaner’s Diamond of Participation (Tash Willcocks on Medium)](https://tash-willcocks.medium.com/the-messy-middle-holding-space-through-kaners-diamond-50e5eeb702bf) — Discusses Sam Kaner’s _Diamond of Participatory Decision-Making_ and how facilitators can hold space during the uncomfortable, divergent-to-convergent "messy middle" phase of group collaborations.
- 📝 **Article**: [When the 3-Sided Polygon Wobbles… The Trust Triangle (Tash Willcocks on Medium)](https://medium.com/@tash-willcocks/when-the-triangle-wobbles-the-trust-triangle-1430e2875c83) — Breaks down Anne Morriss and Frances Frei's Trust Triangle model (Logic, Authenticity, Empathy), explaining how trust breaks when any of these components wobble and how facilitators can maintain balance through active listening and modeled vulnerability.
- 📝 **Article**: [How to Help Your (Human) HiPPO (Tash Willcocks on Medium)](https://medium.com/@tash-willcocks/how-to-help-your-hippo-cb554c4b8aca) — Uses the metaphor of Colombia's invasive hippos to discuss HiPPOs (Highest Paid Person's Opinion) in workshops, offering facilitation tips to channel executive authority into collaborative contributions rather than silencing the room.
- 📝 **Article**: [Drammmmmmma Triangle to Empowerment Dynamic (Tash Willcocks on Medium)](https://medium.com/@tash-willcocks/drammmmmmma-triangle-to-empowerment-dynamic-b491cf47d54b) — Examines Stephen Karpman's Drama Triangle (Victim, Persecutor, Rescuer) and shows how facilitators and teams can pivot to David Emerald's Empowerment Dynamic (TED) to replace anxiety-driven blame with outcome-focused trust.
- 📝 **Article**: [Facilitators… Tell Me What’s Coming Next (Tash Willcocks on Medium)](https://tash-willcocks.medium.com/facilitators-tell-me-whats-coming-next-720534260e2c) — Frames transparent agendas, clear signposting, and chunking instructions as essential forms of participant care that lower cognitive load and cultivate psychological safety.
- 📝 **Article**: [Facilitation: Silence is Golden (Tash Willcocks on Medium)](https://medium.com/@tash-willcocks/facilitation-silence-is-golden-82b72059eff0) — Explores the power of silence in group facilitation. Rather than rushing to fill quiet space (which often reinforces dominant voices), it encourages holding the silence longer to allow deeper thoughts to surface and quieter participants to speak.
- 📝 **Article**: [Feedback Hacky Sack and Other Ways to Not Boot Blame at Someone’s Face (Tash Willcocks on Medium)](https://tash-willcocks.medium.com/feedback-hacky-sack-and-other-ways-to-not-boot-blame-at-someones-face-093c5676cf73) — Proposes a collaborative "hacky sack" metaphor for team communication, encouraging feedback to be passed gently and caught with care, transforming it from a source of evaluation anxiety into a shared learning rhythm.
- 📝 **Article**: [Getting Comfortable with Being Uncomfortable (Tash Willcocks on Medium)](https://tash-willcocks.medium.com/getting-comfortable-with-being-uncomfortable-spoiler-you-dont-58ff49d1fc6e) — Explores discomfort in learning. Referencing the Yerkes-Dodson law, it outlines the "awkward middle" sweet spot between boredom and anxiety where growth occurs, and argues that psychological safety is not about making group work comfortable, but about making the discomfort "survivable".
- 📝 **Article**: [Wheelan Wrapped: The 5 Stages of Team Development (Tash Willcocks on Medium)](https://medium.com/design-bootcamp/wheelan-wrapped-the-5-stages-of-team-development-4746de391ab0) — A practical, conversational breakdown of Susan Wheelan's Integrated Model of Group Development (IMGD), showing how teams transition from dependency to counter-dependency, trust-building, and high performance.
- 📝 **Blog Portal**: [SessionLab Facilitation Resources Blog](https://www.sessionlab.com/blog/) — A curated blog library sharing practical advice on session design, workshop templates, and group dynamics management.
